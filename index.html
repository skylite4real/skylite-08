SKYLite Partnership Requirements

Pre-roll Ads: Ads that play before the main content starts. These ads are shown to viewers before they engage with the video, ensuring that the advertisement is seen before the content begins.

Mid-roll Ads: Ads that appear during the middle of the content, often placed during natural breaks or transitions. They are effective for longer videos as they capture viewers' attention partway through, but they can interrupt the viewing experience.

Post-roll Ads: Ads that play after the main content ends. These ads target viewers who have watched the entire video, making them ideal for engaging those who are already fully engaged with the content.

Banner Ads: Visual ads displayed on the screen, typically at the top or bottom. They can be static or interactive, providing a less intrusive way to display advertisements while viewers consume content.

Skippable Ads: These ads can be skipped by viewers after a few seconds. They are designed to be less intrusive and can provide a better user experience, but they may generate less revenue as viewers might skip them before the ad finishes.

Non-Skippable Ads: These ads must be watched in full before the content plays. They can be more lucrative due to their guaranteed viewership, but they may affect viewer retention by disrupting the content experience.
